Class Features

As an Astromancer, you gain the following class features.

Hit Points

Hit Dice: 1d6 per Astromancer level
Hit Points at 1st Level: 6 + your Constitution modifier
Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d6 (or 4) + your Constitution modifier per astromancer level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: Light Armor
Weapons: Simple weapons, Ranged weapons, quarterstaff
Tools: Navigator’s Tools and One set of artisan’s tools of your choice Saving Throws: Intelligence, Wisdom
Skills: Choose three from Investigation, Perception, Nature, Survival, Sleight of Hand, and Religion

Equipment

You start with the following equipment, in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

(a) any simple weapon or (b) a quarterstaff
(a) a scholar’s pack or (b) a priest’s pack
(a) glassblower’s tools, (b) calligraphy tools, or (b) any other artisan’s tools
Leather armor and a dagger

Spellcasting

1st level astromancer feature

You have learned to use the power of the stars to create, destroy and alter the world around you. See Spells Rules for the general rules of spellcasting and the Spells Listing for the astromancer spell list.

Cantrips

You know two cantrips of your choice from the astromancer spell list. You learn additional astromancer cantrips of your choice at higher levels, as shown in the Cantrips Known column of the Astromancer table.

Preparing and Casting Spells

The Astromancer table shows how many spell slots you have to cast your astromancer spells of 1st level and higher. To cast one of these spells, you must expend a slot of the spell’s level or higher. You regain all expended spell slots when you finish a long rest.

For example, if you know the 1st-level spell cure wounds and have a 1st-level and a 2nd-level spell slot available, you can cast cure wounds using either slot.

You prepare the list of astromancer spells that are available for you to cast, choosing from the astromancer spell list. When you do so, choose a number of druid spells equal to your Wisdom modifier + your astromancer level (minimum of one spell). The spells must be of a level for which you have spell slots.

You can also change your list of prepared spells when you finish a long rest.

Spellcasting Ability

Wisdom is your spellcasting ability for your astromancer spells. Your magic comes from the power and force of the lights that dot the sky. You use your Wisdom whenever a spell refers to your spellcasting ability. In addition, you use your Wisdom modifier when setting the saving throw DC for a druid spell you cast and when making an attack roll with one.

Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier

Ritual Casting

You can cast an astromancer spell as a ritual if that spell has the ritual tag and you have the spell prepared.

Spellcasting Focus

You can use a Spyglass, Glassblower’s Tools, or any arcane focus as a spellcasting focus for your astromancer spells.

Astronomical Process

1st and 7th level astromancer feature

Starting at 1st level you may choose to use Intelligence as your spellcasting ability score instead of Wisdom. If you do so, the following features apply to you:

  • Number of prepared spells = your Intelligence modifier + your astromancer level
  • Spell save DC = 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier
  • Spell attack modifier = your proficiency bonus + your Intelligence modifier
  • You gain proficiency with calligrapher’s tools or any other artisan’s tools if you already have proficiency with calligrapher's tools
  • You know the Magic Missile spell as an astromancer spell and always have it prepared. It does not count against the number of spells you can prepare.
  • At 7th level, you can cast the Far Step spell once per long rest.

Otherwise, the following features apply to you:

  • You gain proficiency with glassblower’s tools or any other artisan’s tools if you already have proficiency with glassblower’s tools
  • You know the Guiding Bolt spell as an astromancer spell and always have it prepared. It does not count against the number of spells you can prepare.
  • At 7th level, you can cast the Telekinesis spell once per long rest

You may change what ability score you use as your spellcasting ability score when you gain the Ability Score Improvement feature at 4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level.

Familiar With The Unknown

2nd level astromancer feature

At 2nd level, your familiarity with the vastness of the universe has hardened your mind against outside forces.

If you are aware of the source of an Intelligence or Wisdom saving throw to charm, frighten, or paralyze you, you have advantage on the roll. You can only gain this benefit while not unconscious.

Astronomical source

3rd level astromancer feature

At 3rd level, you choose a specific source of your astronomical power. Your choice grants you features at 3rd, 6th, 14th and 20th level.

Source Spells

3rd level astromancer feature

At 3rd level, your chosen atromancer source grants you a list of spells at certain class levels. These spells count as astromancer spells for you and are always prepared, but do not count against the number of spells you can prepare.

Abilty Score Improvement

4th, 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level astromancer feature

When you reach 4th level, and again at 8th, 12th, 16th, and 19th level, you can increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or you can increase two ability scores of your choice by 1. As normal, you can’t increase an ability score above 20 using this feature.

Power Cycle

5th, 9th, 13th, and 17th level astromancer feature

Starting at 5th level, when you cast a spell of 3rd level or higher, roll a d12. If the number rolled is a 1 or a 2, you regain a spell slot of that level. At 9th level, the die becomes a d10, at 13th level, the die becomes a d8, and at 17th level, the die becomes a d6. Starting at 9th level, if the number rolled is a 1, you regain hit points equal to your level in this class.

Additionally, whenever you roll hit dice from this class to regain hit points, if you roll a 1 on the die, you regain a spell slot according to your level in this class:
Class Levels 1-7: 1st level spell slot
Class Levels 8-15: 2nd level spell slot
Class Levels 16-20: 3rd level spell slot

Spell slots recovered from this feature may exceed your maximum number of spell slots for each level by 3. However, any excess spell slots that remain at the end of a long rest disappear.

Astronomical Aura

10th level astromancer feature

As a bonus action you cause yourself to emit bright light for 30 ft and dim light for an additional 30 ft. One creature of your choice within the bright light caused by this feature cannot benefit from resistance to radiant or necrotic damage and attack rolls against the creature are granted a +2 bonus.

This lasts for 1 minute or until you cancel it as a bonus action.
You can use this feature a number of times equal to your spellcasting ability modifier (minimum of once).

Astronomical Sources

Astromancers have many options to draw power from. Specializing in a specific source of power can grant special abilities that are not powssible to achieve through normal study.

Dwarf Star

You siphon power from the near everlasting energy of a dwarf star. This energy has granted you the ability to heal and even revive allies.

Slow Burning

3rd level Dwarf Star source feature

Starting at 3rd level when you choose this source, the long life of the source of your power grants you an extended lifespan.
When you roll a 1 on the d20 for a death saving throw, you suffer no failure.

Life Cycle

6th level Dwarf Star source feature

Upon reaching 6th level, you gain the ability to use the excess energy of spells to heal your allies.
When you cast a spell of 3rd level or higher that heals at least one other creature, if the roll of the die from your Power Cycle feature is a 1, you may choose to forgo regaining a spell slot. Istead one creature of your choice that was targeted by the triggering spell regains additional hit points equal to twice your level in this class.

Aura of Longevity

14th level Dwarf Star source feature

At 14th level, your Astronomical Aura begins to aid your allies.
Friendly creatures other than yourself within the bright light of your Astronomical Aura gain advantage on Death Saving Throws. Additionally, a roll of 1 can be counted as a 2 for any spell or effect of your choice that cause a creature to regain hit points, provided the target is within the dim or bright light of your Astronomical Aura.

Neverdeath

20th level Dwarf Star source feature

At 20th level, you tap into the depths of the dwarf star you draw your power from.
While your Astronomical Aura is active, if you are reduced to 0 hit points or outright killed, you can choose to end the Astronomical Aura and stay at 1 hit point. All remaining uses of your Astronomical Aura are depeleted if you do this in response to being outright killed.

If you roll an 18 or higher on the d20 for a death saving throw you regain 1 hit point and become conscious, instantly ending the prone condition.

Hypergiant

You take energy from an explosive and short-lived hypergiant star. The raw power of this type of star has made you adept with powerful, damaging magic.

Quickflame

3rd level Hypergiant source feature

When you use your action to cast a spell of 1st level or higher that deals fire or radiant damage, you may cast a cantrip that has a casting time of 1 action and deals damage as a bonus action.

Fusion Surge

6th level Hypergiant source feature

When you cast an evocation spell of 3rd level or higher that deals damage, you may expend an additional spell slot of the same level that you cast it at to upcast the spell by one level. Targets damaged by a spell cast like this cannot gain benefit from resistance to the damage type of the spell.

Ex: You can expend two 3rd level spell slots as part of one action to cast Fireball at 4th level.

Thorough Flames

14th level Hypergiant source feature

When your Astronomical Aura is active, spells you cast that deal fire or radiant damage deal an additional damage die of their type.

Additionaly, creatures cannot benefit from being invisible within the bright light of your Astronomical Aura and all creatures except you within the bright light suffer disadvantage on dexterity saving throws.

Fire Of The 524th Great Wolf

20th level Hypergiant source feature Whenever you cast a spell that has an area of effect, the range of the spell is doubled and every dimension of the area of effect is doubled. Creatures that have a trait that allows them to take no damage on a successful dexterity saving throw or half damage on a failed dexterity saving throw take the normal amount of damage when you cast a spell with an area of effect.

Additionally, whenever you fail a death saving throw, a 5th level fireball spell detonates centered on you, but does not harm you or your allies.
This fireball is affected by the aforementioned spell area of effect changes.
